Business model

With more than 250 million free accounts in 2019 and the determination not to display advertising to its users, it is interesting to ask how the company is today valued at more than 2 billion dollars.

1. Nitro and Nitro Classic subscriptions

The free part of the application presented in the other sections constitutes the Freemium part of the application and Nitro subscriptions represent the Premium part.

The Nitro Offer :

Offered at 99.99$US per year or 9.99$US per month, Discord's Nitro offer gives access to many additional features while supporting the platform.

These features include the ability to send files, whether photos, videos or audios, up to 100MB, with a limit of 20MB in Freemium. This subscription also gives you the possibility to share your screen with a better quality. For more information on screen sharing, see the functionalities section. We can also mention the possibility to use an animated avatar (.GIF) and as well, to create your own emojis.

Finally, this subscription offers you the possibility to support a server thanks to two server boosts. If you don't know what is a server, I refer you to the functionalities section.The more a server receives boosts from its members, the more it gains in visibility and possibilities (improving the audio quality in the vocal chanels for example).

The Nitro Classic offer

Less expensive than the Nitro offer, the Classic one is available for 49.99$US per year or 4.99$US per month. The Nitro Classic has the same advantages as the Nitro subscription, excluding boosts.

If you would like to find out more about these subscriptions I invite you to visit the official Discord page.For more information about the boosts, I refer you to the boosts explanatory page.

2. Game shop

In order to diversify its activities and sources of incomes, Discord has launched in 2018 its own virtual video game shop, like Steam or the Epic Games Store. Accessible only to Nitro and Nitro Classic, subscribers, this game shop showcases lots of independent games as well as more well-known licences. Discord's strategy for inviting developers to use its shop as a sales platform lies first and foremost in profit sharing.

Indeed, the company proposes a 90/10 remuneration for developers, Discord taking 10% of the profits. The platform's other strategy to attract developers who want to start their business is to offer them the opportunity to launch their game directly on their own server. Users can then purchase the game via the server, give feedbacks to the developers and directly interact with other users of the game. For developers, using Discord allows them to easily update the game to add content or patches but also to see their game appearing on the main page of the Discord shop if it is validated by the platform.

If you want to publish your own game through Discord, I refer you to the official page of the company related to this subject.

3. Personal information and controversy

It must be admitted that despite the presence of paid subscriptions and a game shop, we can still wonder : is it enough, while still offering a free version of the application, to reach a value of more than two billion dollars?

A controversy arose from this question. Is the company, despite its communication advocating proximity to users, selling their personal information ? A detail having all the more aroused users' suspicions, Discord updated its privacy policy in the weeks following the development of this controversy.
It says:

We collect information from you when you voluntarily provide such information, such as when you register for access to the Services or use certain Services. Information we collect may include but not be limited to username, email address, and any messages, images, transient VOIP data (to enable communication delivery only) or other content you send via the chat feature.

Privacy policy

The company concedes that it collect any information sent by its users, so why do people continue to use this platform ?

The answer is quite simple, all users already more or less knew it. Discord mainly attracts gamers and developers, people who are therefore fairly well informed about how the internet works. These users therefore knew that, on Discord or anywhere on the Internet, their information is constantly being collected. What may have encouraged the users to stay was the clarity shown by the company as soon as the controversy arose. They clearly assumed this collection unlike most other companies doing everything possible to conceal it.
